The shop · since 1985

Forty-one years on
the same block. The bench
does not move.

Won Yi opened the shop in 1985 in a 240-square-foot bay off Market between 4th and 5th. He'd apprenticed in Seoul for nine years before that, hand-stitching dress shoes for a maker that doesn't exist anymore. He brought one suitcase to San Francisco and the tools fit inside it.

Four decades later, his son Daniel works the bench three days a week and Won still comes in on Tuesdays — he won't say to do what, exactly, but the regulars know. The awls and the lasts are the same ones. The thread spools have been refilled a thousand times. The bench does not move.

We are not a chain. We are not a "concept." We resole the same boots we resoled for your father when he worked downtown. If you are in your twenties and you bought a pair of boots that cost more than your phone — bring them in before the welt goes. We will tell you what they need. If they don't need anything, we will tell you that, and you can go.

The shoes are made to be repaired. That's why we sell repair, not new shoes. — Won Yi, founder · in an SFGate interview, 2003
